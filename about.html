About

St. John Bosco: The Father of Youth

St. John Bosco, also known as Don Bosco, was an Italian priest, educator, and writer devoted to the well-being of disadvantaged youth. Born in 1815, he founded the Salesian Society, promoting education and kindness as foundations for youth development.

Early Life

John Bosco lost his father at age two. His mother, Margaret, raised him in poverty but with strong faith. He pursued the priesthood despite hardships and was ordained in 1841.

Mission and Philosophy

Don Bosco focused on educating and guiding homeless boys in Turin. His “Preventive System” emphasized reason, religion, and loving kindness rather than punishment.

Salesian Legacy

In 1859, he founded the Salesian Society and later co-founded the Daughters of Mary Help of Christians for girls. His network of schools and homes spread worldwide.

Canonization

He was canonized in 1934. His life’s work continues through global Salesian institutions that provide hope and education to millions of youth.
